01

Break the Silence

Season 1 · 7 guests · The first brave step

+

Season 1 didn't try to solve anything. It tried to make space. Fonterra people told stories they'd never said out loud at work — loss, depression, the weight of carrying it alone. The bravery isn't in the answers; it's in the fact that anyone showed up to answer at all.

A question that cut through
So when was the moment you realised you couldn't keep carrying this on your own?
— Wayne, Host
What this season kept coming back to
silence shame loss weight okay burden kind hidden story unburdened alone
Guest voices
It's okay to not be okay. I mean, it is. So, yeah — be kind to yourself, I think.
Michelle
I just realised that life was just rubbish. I've got to change.
Craig
I should have been more open with Ro. I would just would have started the conversation a lot earlier. It doesn't have to be big before you start talking about an issue. It just feels so much better to have it out.
Rob
There's nothing better than being able to be yourself at work, right? I can be me. And that, if everyone eventually can say that, we've done a bloody good thing here at Fonterra.
Rene
You don't have to have experience in these tough times. Just be there and just try to be there for people.
Sarah
A support network is not someone that just agrees with your view. If we just have everyone that's going to agree with us, then guess what? We're never going to grow.
Jeff
The moment I shared my story with the people who are genuinely close friends to me, I kind of felt unburdened. It was like this weight was lifted off my shoulders — the dirty little secret.
Kathy
02

Identity & Rebuilding

Season 2 · 5 guests · From disclosure to reconstruction

+

If Season 1 was about breaking silence, Season 2 was about what you do next. Guests stopped asking whether they could talk and started asking who they wanted to become afterwards. The stories got more specific, the pain more named, and the hope more earned.

A question that cut through
If you could sit with him now — what would you want him to know?
— Wayne, Host
What this season kept coming back to
identity rebuild father balance open up normal together messy honest love vibe
Guest voices
I would tell him that I love him. That would be the first thing I would say. And the second would be — I know that life isn't going well, but I promise that together we can make it better.
Kahla
If you open up, if you start the conversation with someone, get the vibe of how they are, they'll open up to you, you can open up to them, and it makes for an easy conversation.
Rohan
I love and inspire to be that balance. Work or at home, you're the same person. I know I'm not. And I'm working on that because there's a good person on both sides.
Mark
She said — you know what you're going through, what you've been through is completely normal. And it's okay to get emotional about it.
Pat
If I can't have this conversation with someone that I've known for the better part of 20 years, why can't I have it with someone that I've known for the better part of 20 seconds?
Pete
03

Tools & Prevention

Season 3 · 6 guests · From story to practice

+

Season 3 pivoted. Instead of "here's what happened to me," guests brought something closer to "here's what actually helps." Financial wellbeing, physical health, stoic thinking, self-kindness. The podcast stopped being only a confessional and started being a toolkit.

A question that cut through
So what's the smallest thing someone could do tomorrow that would actually make a difference?
— Wayne, Host
What this season kept coming back to
practice prevent savings spot fire stoic kind physical objective hard things ask tools
Guest voices
It's much easier to put out the spot fires as they're coming up, than wait till your house is burning down.
Belinda
I would say more of what I do is about the mental health side and emotional side, but what comes from that are really good physical results.
Diane
Doing hard things sometimes, you know, helps us when we face other challenges in life.
Tim
We do have to be kind to ourselves.
Vicki
The easiest place for you to improve your savings capacity is to gain a pay rise. And to bank it. But a lot of people are scared to ask.
Steve
I argue, Wayne, that we actually can't be objective about our own lives at any time. No matter how emotionally intelligent you think you might be — please don't fall into that trap.
Cate
04

Leadership as Practice

Season 4 · 6 guests · The mirror turns to leaders

+

By Season 4, the conversation matured into something harder. It wasn't "be a better leader" — it was "the way you show up is already teaching your team what's allowed." Silence as a leadership act. Role-modelling as the real policy. The bravest move yet: asking leaders to see themselves.

A question that cut through
If your team watched you this week — what would they say you were really telling them was okay?
— Wayne, Host
What this season kept coming back to
role model trust adapt behaviour show up silence freedom box learn right example
Guest voices
People watch to see what you're doing. And people look for role models. You are telling me to go home at a certain time — but you are still working really late.
Suzie
Credibility comes through continuing to learn and adapt. Earning trust is about continuing to show up, delivering on promises and being pragmatic.
Justin
As leaders we forget that our behaviours actually show other people how they need to show up. You are setting the example that everybody else should show up as.
Renee
You spend more time keeping the walls of the box up than dealing with the issue. If you've got the freedom to deal with the issue, you can turn to a positive approach.
Brent
It comes back to being right — and it's a silly way to live.
Jorja
Quite often the most powerful thing to do as a leader is to not speak at all.
David

Four seasons in, the conversation isn't winding down. It's opening up.

What started as breaking the silence has become a practice — one the business keeps getting better at, one episode at a time.
